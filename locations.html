Locations

South Carolina is home to several major nuclear facilities that generate electricity and support national energy and security efforts. Below is a list of the state’s primary nuclear energy facilities, their locations by county, and a brief description of each.

Nuclear Energy Facilities in South Carolina

Oconee Nuclear Station

Oconee County

Oconee Nuclear Station consists of three nuclear reactors and is one of the largest nuclear power plants in the state. Operated by Duke Energy, the facility has been generating electricity since the early 1970s. It provides reliable, carbon-free power to hundreds of thousands of homes across South Carolina and neighboring states.

Catawba Nuclear Station

York County

The Catawba Nuclear Station has two reactors and is jointly operated by Duke Energy and North Carolina utilities. Located along the Catawba River, the plant uses water from the river for cooling purposes. It is a major contributor to the region’s electricity supply and plays a key role in grid reliability.

Virgil C. Summer Nuclear Station

Fairfield County

This facility contains one operating reactor that supplies a significant portion of South Carolina’s electricity. Operated by Dominion Energy South Carolina, the plant is located along the Broad River and includes a large cooling tower visible from surrounding areas. The site previously began construction on additional reactors; those projects were later discontinued.

Savannah River Site (SRS)

Aiken, Barnwell, and Allendale Counties

The Savannah River Site is a large federal facility operated by the U.S. Department of Energy. Originally built during the Cold War, SRS focuses on nuclear materials management, environmental cleanup, and national security missions. While it is not a commercial power plant, it plays a critical role in nuclear research, waste management, and defense-related operations.
